# Technical Requirements

## System-Level Requirements

### Operational Requirements
- **Payload Capacity**: Minimum 20 metric tons per climber
- **Transit Time**: Earth surface to GEO in 5-8 days
- **Availability**: 90%+ operational uptime
- **Lifetime**: Minimum 30 years operational life
- **Concurrent Climbers**: Support 10+ climbers simultaneously

### Performance Requirements
- **Maximum Acceleration**: ≤ 3g for crewed missions, ≤ 10g for cargo
- **Climbing Speed**: 100-300 km/h average
- **Safety Factor**: Minimum 2.0 for cable under all load conditions
- **Emergency Descent**: Capability for rapid emergency return

## Cable Requirements

### Material Properties
- **Tensile Strength**: > 50 GPa (gigapascals)
- **Density**: < 2000 kg/m³
- **Specific Strength**: > 25 MN·m/kg (mega-newton meters per kilogram)
- **Fatigue Resistance**: Capable of 10⁹+ load cycles
- **Temperature Range**: -200°C to +200°C operational
- **Radiation Resistance**: Withstand space radiation for 30+ years

### Structural Requirements
- **Total Length**: Approximately 100,000 km (Earth surface to counterweight)
- **Cross-sectional Profile**: Tapered design optimized for stress distribution
- **Maximum Stress Point**: At geostationary orbit altitude (35,786 km)
- **Redundancy**: Multiple parallel strands with independent load paths

## Climber Requirements

### Propulsion System
- **Power Source**: Solar, beamed power, or hybrid
- **Efficiency**: > 70% energy conversion
- **Traction System**: Reliable grip on cable without damage
- **Braking**: Multiple redundant braking systems

### Structural Requirements
- **Mass**: 5-20 metric tons (empty)
- **Volume**: Optimized for payload capacity
- **Life Support**: For crewed missions, 30+ day endurance
- **Power Storage**: Backup power for emergency situations

## Ground Station Requirements

### Location Criteria
- **Latitude**: Equatorial (±5° of equator preferred)
- **Altitude**: Sea level or elevated platform
- **Accessibility**: Ground and sea access for construction and operations
- **Weather**: Minimal severe weather (hurricanes, typhoons)
- **Geopolitics**: Stable jurisdiction with space-friendly regulations

### Infrastructure
- **Platform**: Fixed or mobile (sea-based options)
- **Power Supply**: 100+ MW capacity
- **Payload Processing**: Loading and unloading facilities
- **Control Center**: Mission operations and monitoring
- **Safety Zone**: Exclusion radius around anchor point

## Counterweight Requirements

### Configuration
- **Altitude**: 100,000+ km from Earth's surface
- **Mass**: 1-10 million metric tons
- **Composition**: Captured asteroid, manufactured structure, or hybrid
- **Station Capability**: Optional habitable/functional space station

### Stability
- **Center of Mass**: Beyond geostationary orbit for tension
- **Attitude Control**: Maintain alignment with Earth
- **Collision Avoidance**: Tracking and maneuvering capability

## Safety Requirements

### Structural Safety
- **Cable Monitoring**: Real-time stress and damage detection
- **Weather Monitoring**: Avoid operations in severe conditions
- **Debris Tracking**: Collision avoidance for space debris
- **Emergency Protocols**: Multiple failure mode contingencies

### Operational Safety
- **Redundant Systems**: No single point of failure for critical systems
- **Abort Capability**: Climbers can safely descend in emergencies
- **Communication**: Continuous monitoring and control
- **Access Control**: Prevent unauthorized use or interference

## Environmental Requirements

### Atmospheric Considerations
- **Lightning Protection**: Grounding and lightning arrestors
- **Wind Loading**: Withstand 200+ km/h winds at surface
- **Corrosion Resistance**: Protection from atmospheric and space environments
- **Thermal Management**: Handle temperature extremes and solar heating

### Space Environment
- **Micrometeorite Protection**: Shielding or self-healing capability
- **Atomic Oxygen**: Resistance to erosion in LEO
- **Plasma Environment**: Withstand charged particle interactions
- **Solar Radiation**: UV and particle radiation resistance

## Regulatory Requirements

### International Compliance
- **Outer Space Treaty**: Compliance with international space law
- **Aviation Regulations**: Coordination with air traffic control
- **Environmental Protection**: Minimize ecological impact
- **Liability Framework**: Clear responsibility and insurance

### Technical Standards
- **Quality Assurance**: Rigorous testing and certification
- **Documentation**: Complete design and operational documentation
- **Inspection Regime**: Regular structural integrity verification
- **Maintenance Protocols**: Scheduled maintenance and repair procedures

## Economic Requirements

### Cost Targets
- **Construction Cost**: < $20 billion total investment
- **Operational Cost**: < $500/kg to GEO
- **Maintenance Cost**: < 10% of operational revenue annually
- **Payback Period**: 15-20 years maximum

### Business Model
- **Revenue Streams**: Cargo launch, crew transport, tourism, research
- **Market Analysis**: Sufficient demand to justify investment
- **Risk Assessment**: Acceptable financial risk profile
- **Funding Strategy**: Phased investment with milestone validation

## Future Extensibility

The design should accommodate:
- Increased capacity through parallel cables or cable strengthening
- Extended reach beyond GEO for lunar missions
- Integration with other space infrastructure
- Technology upgrades without complete system replacement
